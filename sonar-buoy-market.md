<p><p>The sonar buoy market is primarily divided into two types: active sonar buoys and passive sonar buoys. Active sonar buoys emit sound waves to detect objects underwater, enabling real-time tracking and targeting, primarily used in military applications. In contrast, passive sonar buoys listen for sounds generated by submarines and marine life, thereby providing stealthy surveillance capabilities. Both types play crucial roles in maritime defense, anti-submarine warfare, and ecological monitoring, catering to diverse operational needs in marine environments.</p></p>
