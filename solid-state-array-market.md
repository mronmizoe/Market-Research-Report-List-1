<p><p>The solid-state array market primarily features three types of memory: MLC (Multi-Level Cell), eMLC (Enterprise Multi-Level Cell), and SLC (Single-Level Cell). MLC stores multiple bits per cell, offering higher capacity and lower cost but with reduced endurance. eMLC enhances MLC performance and endurance for enterprise applications, balancing cost and reliability. SLC, storing a single bit per cell, delivers superior durability and speed, making it ideal for high-performance tasks, albeit at a higher price point. Each type serves distinct needs in the market.</p></p>
