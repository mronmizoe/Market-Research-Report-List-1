<p><p>The special graphite market comprises several key types: Isotropic graphite exhibits uniform properties in all directions, making it ideal for high-performance applications. Extruded graphite is produced through a continuous process, offering high density and strength, suitable for electrical contacts and electrodes. Molded graphite is formed under high pressure, resulting in intricate shapes and high thermal conductivity, commonly used in industrial applications. Other forms include blended and natural graphite, catering to diverse needs across sectors like aerospace, electronics, and energy.</p></p>
